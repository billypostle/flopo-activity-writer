# Feelings in winter
## Concept

---

**Objective**

- children to link how the winter season makes them feel emotional
- How a season makes our bodies feel
- Explore what we wear in winter to keep warm

---

**Materials needed**

- emotion cards
- Pencil and paper
- Insets of shapes/ shapes / stencils
- Diagrams of bodies
- Winter clothes
- baskets

---

**Set-up**

- gather resources
- place pencils and paper on the table
- Place emotion cards on the table in a basket or scattered

---

**Adult role**

- provide the opportunity for children to share thoughts and feelings and log them
- Scaffold sharing waiting and turn taking
- Support physical development when using writing material
- Help children learn their shapes and extend on current knowledge on shapes
- Allow the freedom for children to discuss emotions and help link them physical feelings to emotions

---

**Observation cues:**

- do the children know how to link physical feelings with emotions
- Can the children use writing material appropriately do they struggle / do they need more support on fine motor skills
- What shapes do the children know
- What emotions do the children already know
- Do the children understand the winter season
- do the children understand the human body

---

**Safety considerations:**

- pencils are not too sharp
- No material is broken when setting up

---

**Links to EYFS (2024) learning outcomes**

- literacy- developing the children’s mark making skills
- Personal, emotional and social development: exploring and extending emotions and how things make us feel
- Understanding of the world - our body functions and the way it makes us feel
