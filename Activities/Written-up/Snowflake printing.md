# Snowflake printing
## Concept

---

**Objective**

- Create repeating snowflake patterns using stamps
- Explore different lengths
- Explore different shades and gradients of a colour/ colours

---

**Materials needed**

- Paper/ card
- Paint (choose if your going for 1 colour with the separate gradients)
- Snowflakes sponges/ stamps
- Washing bowl to clean stamps
- Paint brushes
- Paint pots
- Messy mat
- Painting trays / paint pots depending on what you choose
- Provide images of snowflakes

---

**Set-up**

- Set up your mat on the table
- Mix your paints to ensure you have the different colours you want
- Place in either paint pots or paint trays
- Place your paper/ card at the table settings
- Scatter your sponges / stamps on the table as well as paint brushes
- Place a warm bowl of water to clean and wash sponges

---

**Adult role**

- Provide enough resources
- To enable children to freely create more then one painting
- Facilitate the freedom to move around
- Encourage mathematical vocabulary
- Develop and extend current knowledge on snowflakes
- Help explore patterns and shapes to develop the activity further
- Encourage imagination within the activity- allow adaptation

---

**Observation cues:**

- How long the children are engaged for
- What the children are most interested in
- Do the children engage with one another
- Do the children negotiate, collaborate or experience conflict over resources
- Facial expressions gestures and emotional responses
- What vocabulary do the children already have or does it need to be extended further
- How do the children handle writing material
- Do the children feel the need create on a larger scale
- Do they ask for further resources
- Are the children problem solving - using their imagination

---

**Links to EYFS (2024) learning outcomes:**

- Expressive arts and design - exploring colours, using their imagination to create
- Understand of the world - exploring natures seasons
- mathematics- looking at different shapes and pattens, incorporating talks around numbers
- Literacy- creating mark that hold meaning

---

**Alternative ethos adaptations**

Forest school- frozen nature art - Make ice discs with natural items.

Montessori-  Number Snowflakes- Place numbered snowflakes in order.

Reggio Emilia - Snowflake Exploration- Cut paper snowflakes & discuss symmetry.

Steiner - water colour painting - explore winter tones softly
