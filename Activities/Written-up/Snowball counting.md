# Snowball counting
## Concept

---

**Objective**

- For children to explore counting
- Sort and catorgorise objects in terms of size
- To share and turn take

---

**Materials needed**

- A set of number cards 0-10, 11-20
- Foam white balls in differentiating sizes
- Baskets for sorting
- Labels small, medium, large
- 4 baskets /buckets

---

**Set-up:**

- Set out 3 empty buckets/ baskets labelled clearly
- Fill the remaining 1 with all sized snow balls
- Have your number cards to hand when needed

---

**Adult role**

- To encourage the use of numbers
- To model the use of language based on size
- Develop their self esteem and confidence
- Facilitate turn taking, waiting and patience
- Observe and only step in to help resolve conflict or problem solve

---

**Observation cues:**

- Understand what numbers the children can recognise
- Do they use small, medium or large or is it more tiny, big etc
- What numbers can the children count to
- Is it an activity that keeps their focus for a short or long period
- Are the children patient in waiting their turn or is the wait too lengthy
- Do the children help one another or is their conflicts
- Watch for emotional responses from children; frustrated, proud etc
- How do the children problem solve and solve solutions

---

**Safety considerations:**

- Ensure the fake snowballs are soft
- Ensure the safety of other children that children are not throwing the snowballs
- The snowballs are child safe and not a chocking hazard

---

**Links to EYFS (2024) learning outcomes:**

- Maths - to develop their understanding on the concept of size, encourage the development of number recognition
- Personal social and emotional development: working on sharing and turn taking skills

---

**Alternative ethos adaptations**

Forest school- winter mud kitchen- Create winter potions with soil, water, twigs.

Montessori-  pouring snow- Pour ice or faux snow using scoops.
