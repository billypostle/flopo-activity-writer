## Concept

**Objective**

- To build children vocabulary
- How to identify weather
- Understand seasons, climate and change
- Learning how to Observe and record
- Work on their mark making skills

---

**Materials needed**

- Paper and pencils
- Clipboard’s
- Images of weather (use a ipad/ tablet to display weather)
- Interactive weather board
- Binoculars

---

**Set-up**

- Set up a space with cushions
- Place all items on a table for them to gather before they come a sit down
- Lay the weather cards out on a mat or rug or use an easel to display the cards
- If using provide the interactive weather board for them to monitor their findings

---

**Adult role**

- Provide resources and help extend their knowledge
- Build on vocabulary
- Facilitate the freedom of movement to explore
- Build positive self esteem in their abilities
- facilitate a space for use of imagination
- helping those to share and take turns

---

**Observation cues:**

- Observe what the children find most interesting and what holds their attention
- How the children interact with one another do they help and support, are they happy to share
- what is the children’s existing knowledge. Do they extend in conversation using descriptive words
- Do the children prefer sitting, standing, walking around
- Do they like working as a team or solo

---

**Safety considerations:**

- Ensure the weather is okay to sit outside eg not too windy and dangerous or lashing down with rain
- The pencils are not too sharp
- No broken resources
- The children know how to use clipboards to ensure no accidents occur

---

**Links to EYFS (2024) learning outcomes**

- Maths - sorting and categorising weather symbols
- Understanding of the world- understanding and acknowledging the seasons, exploring nature
- Literacy- creating marks on paper
- Communication and language - using visual cards and developing on existing vocabulary
