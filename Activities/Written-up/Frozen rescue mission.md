# Frozen rescue mission
## Concept

---

**Objective:**

- Children to explore the concept of cause and effect
- Understand the difference between solids and liquids
- How to reverse freezing
- Develop hand eye co-ordination
- Learning about animal habitats

---

**Materials needed**

- Tuff tray / plastic trays
- Pipettes
- X2 Bowls of warm water
- Freezer
- tupaware
- Winter animal figures
- Natural resources (leaves, twigs, flowers

---

**Set-up**

- Clean the tuff tray
- Lay down your base to the tray this is using your natural winter resources
- Place your ice blocks around the tray at arms length
- Place your pipettes around the outside of the tray
- Place your refill warm water bowls outside of the tray on a neighbouring table to avoid spillage

---

**Adult role**

- To provide enough resources for the group
- To extend their knowledge on the cause and effect concept
- Developing their understanding on material status
- Support sharing of resources and support conflict that may occur
- Provide new vocabulary
- Support their independence and self confidence

---

**Observation cues:**

- What duration of time is spent at the activity. Are the children engaged?
- What resources create the most amount of interest .e. use of pipettes etc
- Emotional responses towards the activity
- What behaviours are present do the children share or work together nicely
- What language is being used
- What is the current knowledge the children have and what needs to be extended
- Do the children struggle with the pipettes (what do they need practice more of to improve mobility
- What ways do the children try and solve the rescue mission - looking for imagination

---

**Safety considerations:**

- Ensure any spillages are mopped straight away to avoid any trips and falls
- Where children are using the ice ensure they are not getting too cold
- Ensure the temperature of water used is warm and not hot (caution of burning)
- No resources are broken or split
- Demonstrate hoe to use pipette to reduce the risk of children catching their fingers

---

**Links to EYFS (2024) learning outcomes:**

Understanding of the world- learning about the seasons, natural winter resources, understanding the frozen elements, learning about habitats and animals

Physical- developing hand eye co-odinatoin with resources

---

**Alternative ethos adaptations**
