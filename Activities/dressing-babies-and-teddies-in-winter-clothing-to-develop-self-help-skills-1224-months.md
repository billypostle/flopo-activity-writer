## Activity Title

Dressing babies and teddies in winter clothing to develop self-help skills (12–24 months)

## Themes

Winter; Montessori; Dressing; All about me

## Activity Summary

This activity invites children to practice dressing a teddy or baby doll in winter clothes. Using child-sized garments and simple fasteners, children explore clothing as they develop hand-eye coordination, patience and self-help skills. Adults support by modelling language, encouraging independent attempts and turning dressing into a playful routine that foregrounds the child’s choices.

## Learning Objectives

This activity supports the development of fine motor skills and hand-eye coordination as children manipulate buttons, zips and fasteners. It fosters independence, self-confidence and a sense of competence as children dress the teddy, with language to request help and describe actions. It also introduces basic weather concepts and clothing choices, while providing opportunities for turn-taking and social interaction.

## Observation Cues

Child selects clothing items and brings them to the teddy. Child uses a pincer grip to handle small fasteners and attempts to fasten with or without help. Child talks or signs about clothing and weather. Child mirrors the teddy in a mirror while dressing. Peer interactions include offering a clothing item or taking turns.

## Materials

Child-sized winter clothing set (coat, jumper, trousers/leggings, hat, scarf, mittens, boots); Teddy or baby doll; Clothing with simple fasteners (large buttons, Velcro, easy zips); Dressing frames or a small dressing mat; Mirror; Low table or mat; Storage tray for clothes

## Linked materials

Doll or teddy; Child-sized winter clothes with simple fasteners; Dressing frames; Mirror; Low table or dressing mat; Safe storage tray for outfits

## Step-by-Step Guidance

1. Arrange a safe, comfortable dressing area on a low mat or table with the teddy placed at the center. 2. Show the teddy a simple outfit and name each item aloud. 3. Offer the child a choice of two items (e.g., two hats) and let them select. 4. Demonstrate one simple action at a time (e.g., arm through sleeve), using short, clear language. 5. Pause for the child to imitate, then provide minimal support as needed. 6. Gradually remove items and repeat with new clothing, cycling through a full outfit if the child is ready. 7. Encourage turn-taking and sharing of clothes with peers, and provide positive language regardless of outcome. 8. When completed, tidy away outfits and reflect briefly with the child. 9. If a child becomes frustrated, switch to a shorter sequence or offer a snack break before trying again. 10. Close with a brief verbal recap and a pretend weather check for the teddy’s new outfit.

## Adult Role

Model simple dressing actions with calm, descriptive language. Offer choices and celebrate attempts, not just successes. Provide one-step prompts and gradually reduce support as the child gains competence. Maintain a steady, patient pace and ensure safety throughout. Observe the child’s problem-solving, language use and independence, stepping in only to ensure safety or extend learning with gentle questions.

## Age Adaptation: 12-24 months (Early Explorers)

Children may begin to imitate dressing actions, select items from a limited choice, and use simple fasteners with verbal prompts or gestures. The adult offers gentle guidance for arm and leg placement, uses short phrases and supports with one hand where needed. Emphasise repetitive practice to build muscle memory and confidence, and use mirrors to support self-recognition and motivation.

## Age Adaptation: 2 years (Budding Adventurers)

Children can choose between several outfits, attempt multiple steps (e.g., put on coat, fasten large buttons, slip on hat), and verbalise needs or preferences. The adult provides scaffolded prompts (one instruction at a time) and reinforces autonomy with independent dressing attempts, while modelling turn-taking and sharing of clothing items.

## Age Adaptation: 3 years (Curious Investigators)

Children independently select clothing, sequence steps (sock, then shoe; arm through sleeve; zip or Velcro), and verbalise reasoning (e.g., weather context, appropriate clothing). The adult offers minimal interference, expands vocabulary around clothing and weather, and provides opportunities for peer collaboration and early self-checks (e.g., “Is that the right sleeve?”).

## Age Adaptation: 4 years (Confident Discoverers)

Children manage most steps unaided, troubleshoot fit and fasteners, and compare outfits for weather appropriateness. The adult roles shifts to facilitator of conversation, encouragement of cooperative dressing with peers, and intentional language to describe mechanisms and outcomes. Additional challenge can include layering and basic sequencing of outfits.

## Space Required

Indoor space with a clear, flat surface or mat; low mirror at child height; a small shelf or tray for outfits; open floor area to allow peers to join without crowding

## Time Required

15–25 minutes per session

## Safety Considerations

Supervise closely; ensure clothing items have large, safe fasteners without small detachable parts; avoid cords or loose drawstrings; keep the teddy/doll secured on a stable surface; ensure the area is free from trip hazards and all materials are age-appropriate; consider allergies to fabrics and dyes; gentle handling and safe washing of clothing between sessions
