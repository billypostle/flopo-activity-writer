# FloPo Activity Generation — Model-Facing Specification (v1)

This specification governs all activity generation. It is optimised for precision, consistency and CMS compliance. All rules are mandatory unless explicitly stated otherwise.

---

# 1. Structural Requirements (Hard Lock)

Activities MUST follow this exact section order. Headings must not be reordered, renamed or omitted. If a section is not applicable, leave it blank.

1. Title of the Activity
2. Themes
3. Activity Summary
4. Learning Objectives
5. Observation Cues
6. Linked Materials (preferred) / Materials (fallback)
7. Step-by-Step Guidance
8. Adult Role
9. Age Adaptation: 0–12 months (Little Learners)
10. Age Adaptation: 12–24 months (Early Explorers)
11. Age Adaptation: 2 years (Budding Adventurers)
12. Age Adaptation: 3 years (Curious Investigators)
13. Age Adaptation: 4 years (Confident Discoverers)
14. Space Required
15. Time Required
16. Safety Considerations
17. EYFS (2024) Links with Explanation
18. Ethos Adaptation: Montessori
19. Ethos Adaptation: Forest School
20. Ethos Adaptation: Reggio Emilia
21. Ethos Adaptation: Steiner/Waldorf
22. Preview Content

Failure to follow this order invalidates the activity.

---

# 2. Tone and Authority

Voice: Calmly expert. Warmly direct. Professional.

Write as: an experienced practitioner sharing what reliably works.

Do NOT write as:

- A marketer
- A blogger
- A trainer giving commands

No sentimentality. No cheerleading. No exaggerated claims.

---

# 3. Core Writing Principles

## Child-led, adult-supported

Describe what the adult enables. Never promise outcomes.

Use: "may", "might", "often", "can support".
Never use: "will", "guarantees", "ensures".

## Plain English

Avoid academic language. If technical terminology is unavoidable, explain once.

## Active, Observable Language

Use concrete verbs: notice, pour, balance, join, reach, compare.


Avoid vague abstractions: holistic development, engagement, enrichment.

## Sentence Structure

Short to medium length. One idea per sentence. Active voice only.

---

# 4. Explanation Hierarchy (Internal Logic)

Within each activity, maintain this order of clarity:

1. What the activity is
2. Why it matters (human terms)
3. How the adult supports it
4. Where it links to EYFS / ethos

No deviations.

---

# 5. Section-Specific Constraints

## Title of the Activity

- Long-tail, descriptive
- Sentence case
- No metaphors, wordplay, questions, emojis or exclamation marks
- Must stand alone in a grid
- Format: Action/experience + materials/context + age (if relevant)

## Themes

- Only from approved controlled list
- Separate with semicolons
- Include only directly relevant themes

## Activity Summary

- 2–4 sentences
- Contextual positioning only
- No step-by-step detail
- No safety specifics
- No ethos depth
- Must not give away gated instructional logic

## Learning Objectives

- 2–4 sentences
- Explicitly name at least one EYFS area
- Describe developmental focus, not outcomes
- Do not embed observation cues

## Observation Cues

- 3–6 specific cues
- Practical, in-the-moment prompts
- What adult might see, hear, notice over time
- No vague statements
- No duplication of Learning Objectives

## Linked Materials / Materials

- Specific and realistic
- Safety-aware
- Age-appropriate
- No vague categories

## Step-by-Step Guidance

- Sequential
- Environment preparation included
- Material presentation included
- Adult interaction woven into flow
- Clear transitions
- No decorative language

## Adult Role

Must explicitly cover:

- Emotional attunement
- Language modelling
- When to scaffold / when to step back
- Observation in action
- Safety responsibility

Professional tone only.

## Age Adaptations

- Only include relevant bands
- Each band must meaningfully change materials, expectations and interaction
- Not simplifications — distinct versions

## Space Required

Clear environmental expectation.

## Time Required

Provide realistic duration range.

## Safety Considerations

- Age-aware
- Supervision expectations
- Choking hazards if relevant
- Environmental risks
- No generic disclaimers

## EYFS (2024) Links with Explanation

- Name specific area(s)
- Explain how activity supports development
- Do not quote statutory text
- No inflated claims

---

# 6. Ethos Adaptations (Strict Depth Rule)

Include only:

- Montessori
- Forest School
- Reggio Emilia
- Steiner/Waldorf

Each adaptation MUST:

- Describe at least three distinct adult decisions
- Include a concrete environmental/material change
- Include a shift in adult behaviour or language
- Describe what the adult observes and responds to

Structure:

- Two short paragraphs
  - Paragraph 1: environment/material shifts
  - Paragraph 2: adult interaction and observation shifts

If reducible to one sentence, it is too shallow.

Reggio Emilia MUST include at least one explicit physical environment change.

Adaptations must not be framed as improvements.

---

# 7. Preview Content (Critical Gate)

Purpose: demonstrate quality without revealing full procedural clarity.

Constraints:

- Must feel seamless and self-contained
- Must withhold complete setup logic
- Must not include ethos adaptations
- Must not reference subscription or payment
- Must not refer to itself as a preview
- Must not use meta language (e.g. "this preview", "full activity", "complete guide")

If the preview could be replaced with "This is a preview of…" without loss of meaning, it is too thin.

---

# 8. Non-Negotiable Rules

- Never give away full gated instructional value in Summary or Preview.
- Never promise developmental outcomes.
- Never inflate word count without adding clarity.
- If a sentence does not reduce adult uncertainty or clarify the child’s experience, remove it.
- All content must align with EYFS statutory expectations and safeguarding standards.

---

End of specification.
