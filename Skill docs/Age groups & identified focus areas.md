# Age groups & identified focus areas

---

# **🌱 Little Learners (0–12 months)**

At this stage, learning is entirely physical and sensory. Outcomes are about foundations, not skills.

<aside>
🔑

**Key Theme:** Secure attachment, sensory exploration, body awareness

</aside>

## **Focus Areas**

- Sensory processing (touch, sound, movement, sight)
- Attachment and emotional security
- Early communication (pre-verbal)

## **Intended Outcomes**

### Children will:

- Develop secure relationships with caregivers
- Begin to respond to voices, facial expressions, and sounds
- Explore the world through movement and senses
- Show increasing control of head, hands, and body
- Experience cause and effect (“when I move, something happens”)

### **What Activities Should Emphasise**

- Tummy time, reaching, grasping, rolling
- Sensory baskets, textures, light and shadow
- Singing, talking, mirroring expressions
- Calm routines and predictable experiences

✔ EYFS alignment: PSED, Physical Development, Communication & Language

---

# **🌿 Early Explorers (12–24 months)**

Toddlers are driven by physical exploration and early autonomy.

<aside>
🔑

**Key Theme:** Independence, movement, curiosity

</aside>

## **Focus Areas**

- Gross motor development
- Early expressive language
- Independence and self-awareness

## **Intended Outcomes**

### Children will:

- Walk, climb, push, pull, and carry with confidence
- Use single words and simple phrases
- Begin to make choices and show preferences
- Explore objects with purpose (posting, stacking, filling)
- Experience early problem-solving through play

### **What Activities Should Emphasise**

- Open-ended physical play
- Simple sensory and heuristic play
- Naming objects, actions, and emotions
- Repetition and routine-based activities
- Opportunities to “do it myself”

✔ EYFS alignment: Prime areas dominate; early Maths & Understanding the World introduced

# **🌼 Budding Adventurers (2 years)**

This is a huge developmental leap year.

<aside>
🔑

**Key Theme:** Assertion, language explosion, social awareness

</aside>

## **Focus Areas**

- Language development
- Emotional regulation
- Pretend play and imitation

## **Intended Outcomes**

### Children will:

- Use two- to three-word sentences
- Begin to express needs, wants, and feelings
- Show interest in other children (parallel play → early interaction)
- Develop early self-regulation (with adult support)
- Engage in imaginative and role play

### **What Activities Should Emphasise**

- Small-world and role-play scenarios
- Songs, stories, and repetition
- Simple turn-taking games
- Naming emotions and modelling calm behaviour
- Hands-on maths concepts (big/little, one/many)

✔ EYFS alignment: Strong PSED focus + early Literacy & Maths foundations

---

# **🔍 Curious Investigators (3 years)**

Three-year-olds are natural researchers.

<aside>
🔑

**Key Theme:** Questioning, thinking, social learning

</aside>

## **Focus Areas**

- Curiosity and questioning
- Cooperative play
- Early literacy and numeracy concepts

## **Intended Outcomes**

### Children will:

- Ask “why” and “how” questions
- Follow simple instructions with multiple steps
- Play cooperatively and begin to negotiate
- Recognise patterns, quantities, and shapes
- Show interest in books, mark-making, and storytelling

### **What Activities Should Emphasise**

- Simple investigations and experiments
- Open-ended questions (“What do you think will happen?”)
- Story sequencing and discussion
- Counting through play
- Creative expression with choice and autonomy

✔ EYFS alignment: Balanced Prime + Specific Areas

---

# **🌟 Confident Discoverers (4 years)**

This stage prepares children for Reception, without being school-like.

<aside>
🔑

**Key Theme:** Confidence, independence, readiness for transition

</aside>

## **Focus Areas**

- Self-confidence and independence
- Applied learning
- Emotional resilience

## **Intended Outcomes**

### Children will:

- Communicate confidently with adults and peers
- Manage personal needs independently
- Apply early literacy and maths in play
- Show sustained focus and perseverance
- Reflect on experiences and express opinions

### **What Activities Should Emphasise**

- Problem-solving challenges
- Collaborative projects
- Story creation and early writing
- Real-world maths (measuring, counting, sharing)
- Emotional literacy and confidence-building
