## Purpose
Generate a **[[Steiner (Waldorf) Research|Steiner (Waldorf)]] ethos adaptation** for any activity that:
- Meaningfully alters **environment, materials, and adult role**
- Remains fully **EYFS-compliant**
- Integrates cleanly into the FloPo activity schema
- Avoids superficial or decorative references

Aligned with FloPo constraints: professional planning tool, low cognitive load, no fluff, no pseudo-philosophy

---

## Core Steiner Principles (Operationalised)
Translate philosophy into observable execution rules:

1. **Imitation over instruction**
   - Adult demonstrates quietly
   - No step-by-step verbal direction unless required for safety

2. **Rhythm and repetition**
   - Activity has predictable flow (setup → exploration → tidy)
   - Repetition is expected and supported, not varied for novelty

3. **Natural materials only**
   - Wood, wool, cotton, silk, metal, water, sand
   - No plastic, bright synthetic colours, or noisy/light-up elements

4. **Open-ended outcome**
   - No fixed “correct” result
   - Avoid product-driven success criteria

5. **Sensory integrity**
   - Materials chosen for authentic texture, weight, temperature
   - Avoid mixed sensory overload (limit to 1–2 dominant sensory qualities)

6. **Calm, uncluttered environment**
   - Limited materials presented at once
   - Neutral or soft colour palette

7. **Adult as anchor, not instructor**
   - Present, observant, minimal language
   - Intervenes only to support engagement or safety

---

## Transformation Rules (What Must Change)
A valid Steiner adaptation must modify at least **3 of the following 4 dimensions**:

### 1. Materials
- Replace synthetic or bright materials with natural equivalents
- Reduce quantity to essentials only

### 2. Environment Setup
- Simplify layout
- Introduce calm, contained workspace (tray, cloth, defined area)

### 3. Adult Behaviour
- Remove directive language
- Replace with modelling and observation

### 4. Activity Structure
- Remove rigid steps where possible
- Allow child-led pacing and repetition

If fewer than 3 dimensions change → reject output (too superficial)

---

## Output Structure (Strict)

**Heading:** Steiner (Waldorf) Adaptation

**Format:** [[Writing guide#Adaptations should usually be **two short paragraphs**|2 paragraphs]] (no bullets, no lists)

**Content order (fixed):**
1. Environment framing
2. Materials adjustment
3. Adult role
4. Child interaction pattern

---

## Writing Constraints

- No mention of “Steiner philosophy says” or similar
- No abstract language (e.g. “holistic”, “nurturing the soul”)
- No outcome claims (e.g. “supports creativity” without observable link)
- No instructions that contradict EYFS safeguarding
- No additional sections or expansion beyond adaptation

Tone:
- Neutral, observational, precise
- Describes what happens, not what it represents

---

## Allowed Language Patterns

Use:
- “The materials are arranged…”
- “The adult demonstrates…”
- “The child returns to…”
- “Only a small number of items are provided…”

Avoid:
- “Encourage the child to…”
- “Ask the child…”
- “Explain that…”

## Example (Reference Only — Do Not Copy)

A small, clearly marked patch of the open grassy space is set aside with a neutral cloth laid as the launch base; only the kite, a spool of natural twine, and a single wind indicator are placed on the cloth to keep the area uncluttered. The kite is made from cotton or silk with a light wooden frame and a cotton tail; wind indicators are wool streamers or natural-feather markers. Sunscreen, hats and water remain available at the edge for safety. 

The adult demonstrates a simple, calm launch and steady handling of the string without step-by-step instruction, remaining close but quiet and observant. The child returns to running with the kite, holding the twine, and watching the wool streamer, naming wind and weather words as they play; the adult intervenes only for safety or to model a steady movement, allowing repeated, child-led trials and pauses.

## Failure Conditions (Auto-Reject)

Reject generation if:
- Contains plastic, bright colours, or branded materials
- Includes step-by-step teaching language
- Introduces a fixed outcome or product
- Reads as generic EYFS guidance with “natural materials” swapped in
- Mentions the word “Steiner” in explanatory tone

---

## Integration Notes (FloPo Schema)

- Must sit under **Ethos Adaptations section**
- Must not alter:
  - Learning objective
  - Safety guidance
  - EYFS alignment

- Must feel like a **credible alternative execution**, not a variant suggestion

---

## Quality Benchmark

Pass if:
- A practitioner could run the activity differently after reading
- The adult role is clearly reduced and observational
- The environment is visibly calmer and simplified

Fail if:
- Only materials changed
- Language remains directive
- Outcome still implied or assessed

---

## Compression Rule (For Generator Efficiency)

Target length: 80–120 words
Single paragraph only
No redundancy or repeated phrasing
