## Purpose

This document defines how to generate **[[Montessori Research|Montessori]] ethos adaptations** that integrate cleanly into the existing FloPo activity schema.

It does not change the core activity.
It defines how the *same activity* is reshaped through a Montessori lens.

All outputs must adhere to:
- Existing activity structure and CMS order (see Activity Structure)
- Writing tone, clarity, and constraints (see Writing Guide)
- EYFS alignment and professional positioning (see Strategic Grounding)

---

## Core Principle

Montessori adaptations are not aesthetic or philosophical summaries.

They are **specific changes to adult decisions** that result in:
- Increased child independence
- Reduced adult intervention
- Clear material logic

If those three shifts are not present, the adaptation is invalid.

---

## Structural Output Requirements

Each Montessori adaptation must:

- Be written as **two short paragraphs**
- Follow the Three I’s implicitly (Intent → Implementation → Impact)
- Contain **at least three distinct adult decisions**
- Be skippable without breaking the main activity

### Paragraph 1: Environment and Materials

Must describe:
- How materials are reduced, ordered, or refined
- How the environment is prepared for independent access
- How the activity is simplified to isolate a key skill

### Paragraph 2: Adult Behaviour and Observation

Must describe:
- How the adult introduces the activity (brief, controlled demonstration)
- How the adult withdraws or limits language
- What the adult observes instead of directing

---

## Required Decision Types (Minimum Coverage)

Every adaptation must clearly include:

### 1. Environment or Setup Change

Examples:
- Materials placed on a tray or defined workspace
- Items arranged in order of use
- Reduced quantity of materials

### 2. Adult Behaviour Shift

Examples:
- Demonstrates once, then steps back
- Uses minimal or no verbal instruction during play
- Avoids questioning or prompting

### 3. Observation Focus

Examples:
- Notices repetition or persistence
- Observes precision or control in movement
- Watches for signs of concentration or disengagement

If any of these are missing, the adaptation fails.

---

## Montessori-Specific Rules for Generation

### 1. Reduce, Do Not Add

The adaptation should **remove complexity**, not introduce variation.

Reject if:
- New materials are added without necessity
- The activity becomes more elaborate than the base version

---

### 2. Isolate a Dominant Skill

The adaptation must make the primary learning focus clearer.

Reject if:
- Multiple competing skills are introduced
- The activity becomes broad or unfocused

---

### 3. Prioritise Independence Over Interaction

The adult should not drive the experience.

Reject if:
- The adult is leading dialogue throughout
- The child relies on instructions to continue

---

### 4. Build in Self-Correction (Control of Error)

Where possible, the adaptation should make outcomes visible to the child.

Examples:
- Objects only fit one way
- Spillage shows overfilling
- Misalignment is visually obvious

Reject if:
- Only the adult can identify mistakes

---

### 5. Use Functional Materials

Materials should feel purposeful, not decorative.

Reject if:
- Materials are purely sensory without structure
- Items are presented as a mixed collection without clear use

---

## Language Constraints (Critical)

All writing must follow the FloPo writing guide:

- [[Writing guide#**Plain English over pedagogy**|Plain English only]]
- No philosophical explanation of Montessori
- No claims of superiority or effectiveness
- No abstract language (e.g. "holistic", "engaging")
- No instructions framed as rules

### Specifically avoid:

- "In Montessori, children learn..."
- "This supports independence and confidence" (without observable detail)
- "Encourage the child to..."

---

## Output Pattern (Model Template)

[[Writing guide#Adaptations should usually be **two short paragraphs**|2 paragraphs]]

**Paragraph 1 — Environment and Materials**

Describe how the setup is simplified, ordered, and made accessible for independent use. Focus on material selection, layout, and clarity of purpose.

**Paragraph 2 — Adult Behaviour and Observation**

Describe how the adult introduces the activity briefly, then reduces input. Focus on what the adult watches for rather than what they instruct.

---

## Quality Control Checklist

An adaptation is valid only if:

- It changes how the activity *looks in practice*
- It includes all three decision types (environment, behaviour, observation)
- It reduces adult input rather than increasing it
- It avoids decorative or stylistic references to Montessori
- It could not be reduced to a single sentence without losing meaning

---

## Failure Patterns (Common Issues)

### Surface-Level Adaptation

> "Use natural materials and allow independent play"

Fails because:
- No clear setup change
- No adult behaviour detail
- No observation guidance

---

### Overwritten / Philosophical

> "This approach follows Montessori principles of independence and self-directed learning"

Fails because:
- Explains philosophy instead of changing practice

---

### Adult-Led Disguised as Montessori

> "Guide the child through each step and support where needed"

Fails because:
- Adult remains central to the activity

---

## Calibration Example (Condensed)

**Montessori Adaptation**

Place four buckets on low marked spots or a shallow tray, each labelled and arranged left-to-right; provide one measuring jug with a single visible fill line and one clipboard with a simple tally sheet per child. Remove extra cups and toys so the sequence (place bucket → wait → pour to line → record) is clear and children can reach, pour and return materials independently. 

Show the sequence once with a slow, silent demonstration—place a bucket, set the timer, pour to the fill line and make one mark—then step back and limit language to brief safety reminders only. Watch for repetition, steady pouring that shows self-correction, waiting for the timer, and use of comparative language on the recording sheets rather than directing the child.

---

## Final Constraint

If the adaptation does not materially change:
- the environment
- the adult’s behaviour
- what is being observed

it should not be generated.
