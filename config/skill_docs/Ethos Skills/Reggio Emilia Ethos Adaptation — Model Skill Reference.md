## Purpose
Enable the activity generator to produce **[[Reggio Emilia Research|Reggio Emilia]]-aligned adaptations** that:
- Meaningfully change adult role, environment, and interaction
- Remain fully compliant with EYFS requirements fileciteturn0file0
- Integrate cleanly into the existing activity schema

---

## Success Criteria
An adaptation is valid ONLY if:
1. The adult is repositioned as observer, listener, and co-learner
2. The environment actively shapes behaviour (not just decor change)
3. The activity allows child-led direction or divergence
4. Materials are open-ended and non-prescriptive
5. Observation opportunities are embedded (not added after)
6. No instructional over-scaffolding is introduced

Fail if:
- Adult leads outcomes or controls process
- Activity remains step-driven with fixed result
- Materials dictate a single use
- "Reggio" is applied as aesthetic only

---

## Core Principles → Execution Rules

### 1. Child as Capable Co-Constructor
DO:
- Allow children to define use of materials
- Accept multiple valid outcomes
- Leave gaps in instruction intentionally

DO NOT:
- Predefine what the child must make or achieve
- Correct or steer towards a "right" outcome

---

### 2. Adult as Observer + Co-Researcher
DO:
- Use commentary instead of instruction
- Follow child focus shifts
- Introduce language that extends thinking (not directs it)

FORMAT:
- "I can see..."
- "You’re trying to..."
- "What happens if..."

DO NOT:
- Give step-by-step direction
- Ask closed questions with expected answers

---

### 3. Environment as Third Teacher
DO:
- Pre-arrange space to invite exploration
- Use intentional layout (grouping, spacing, access)
- Ensure materials are visible and reachable

DO NOT:
- Present materials as a single prepared task
- Hide materials or gate access unnecessarily

---

### 4. Open-Ended Materials (Loose Parts Priority)
DO:
- Use natural, varied, reusable materials
- Allow multiple combinations and outcomes

Examples:
- Stones, shells, fabric, wood pieces, metal objects, water, light elements

DO NOT:
- Use single-outcome craft kits
- Use materials that imply one correct method

---

### 5. Process Over Outcome
DO:
- Remove pressure for finished product
- Allow repetition, pause, abandonment, return

DO NOT:
- Frame activity around completion or display outcome

---

### 6. Documentation & Observation (Embedded)
DO:
- Surface moments worth observing within the activity flow
- Highlight behaviours: exploration, problem-solving, communication

DO NOT:
- Add observation as a separate bolt-on section only

---

## Adaptation Construction Template

When generating a Reggio adaptation, modify these components:

### A. Environment Setup (MANDATORY CHANGE)
- Reconfigure layout to encourage self-direction
- Materials arranged openly (not handed out stepwise)

### B. Adult Role (MANDATORY CHANGE)
- Replace instructions with observation prompts
- Remove directive language

### C. Materials (MANDATORY CHECK)
- Convert to open-ended where possible
- Remove overly specific tools if they constrain outcomes

### D. Flow Adjustment (MANDATORY CHANGE)
- Replace sequence with flexible entry points
- Allow multiple pathways through activity

### E. Language Layer (MANDATORY CHANGE)
- Insert reflective commentary examples
- Avoid instructional phrasing

---

## Output Format (For Activity Schema)

Reggio Emilia Adaptation must:
- Be [[Writing guide#Adaptations should usually be **two short paragraphs**|2 paragraphs]]
- Be clearly labelled as an [[Writing guide#Ethos adaptations must|ethos adaptation]]
- Be written as a full re-framing of the activity (not bullet tweaks)
- Maintain same section order as core activity

Structure:
1. Environment shift
2. Materials adjustment
3. Adult role + interaction examples
4. How child direction may emerge
5. Observation cues (embedded)

---

## Language Constraints

Use:
- Neutral, observational tone
- Specific behavioural descriptions

Avoid:
- "Encourage the child to..."
- "Guide the child to..."
- "Teach them to..."
- "The aim is to produce..."

Replace with:
- "The child may..."
- "The adult notices..."
- "This can lead to..."

---

## Edge Case Rules

### If activity is highly structured (e.g. craft)
- Strip back outcome expectation
- Reframe materials as exploratory tools

### If safety constraints exist
- Maintain safeguarding control (non-negotiable)
- Apply freedom within safe boundaries only

### If EYFS objective is narrow
- Expand pathways to reach the same objective via multiple routes

---

## Anti-Patterns (Auto-Fail)

- Adding a single sentence: "Use a Reggio approach"
- Keeping full original steps and adding "allow exploration"
- Over-directing under the guise of questioning
- Aesthetic-only changes (e.g. "use natural materials" with no structural shift)

---

## Minimal Example (Reference)

Base activity: pouring water between containers

Reggio adaptation shift:
- Materials arranged across tray with varied vessels
- No demonstration given
- Adult observes flow, volume, repetition patterns
- Child selects tools and defines use
- Adult comments on changes rather than instructs method

---

## Final Check (Pre-Output)

Reject output if:
- It reads like a standard guided activity
- Adult still controls sequence
- Outcome is implied or fixed
- No meaningful environmental change is present

Accept only if:
- The activity could unfold differently each time
- The adult can step back without breaking the activity
- Observation opportunities are obvious within the flow
