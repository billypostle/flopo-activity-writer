# FloPo -- Strategic Grounding & AGENTS Reference

*(Single Source of Truth for Decisions, Content & Automation)*

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 1. What FloPo Is

FloPo is a UK-based digital resource platform providing structured,
evidence-aligned early years activity content for 0--4 year olds.

Primary audience: - Childminders\
- Nursery practitioners\
- Nannies\
- Parents seeking structured guidance

FloPo provides: - Individual activity guides\
- Themed activity packs\
- Subscription-based gated content\
- Structured EYFS-aligned planning support

All content is delivered digitally (gated CMS-driven content). No
physical products.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 2. Core Positioning

FloPo sits at the intersection of: - Statutory EYFS requirements\
- Ofsted accountability pressures\
- Low-cost accessibility\
- Structured, professional documentation\
- Ethos-informed adaptation (Montessori, Reggio Emilia, Forest School,
Steiner etc.)

FloPo is not: - A Pinterest-style inspiration blog\
- A worksheet-heavy printable shop\
- A craft marketplace\
- A child entertainment brand

FloPo is a professional planning tool.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 3. Regulatory Foundation

All content must align with: - EYFS statutory framework (latest version,
effective September 2025)\
- Seven areas of learning: - Communication & Language - Physical
Development - Personal, Social & Emotional Development - Literacy -
Mathematics - Understanding the World - Expressive Arts & Design -
Safeguarding and welfare awareness\
- Ofsted inspection realities\
- Age-appropriate development (0--24 months and 2--4 structured
separately when required)

FloPo content must never contradict statutory safeguarding or welfare
requirements.

Ethos adaptations must: - Be clearly labelled as adaptations\
- Never override EYFS compliance\
- Avoid implying regulatory equivalence to alternative philosophies

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 4. Product Structure

### Core Unit: Individual Activity

Each activity includes: - Long-tail SEO-optimised title\
- Age range\
- Clear learning objective linked to EYFS area(s)\
- Observation cues embedded within learning objective context\
- Materials (realistic, setting-appropriate)\
- Step-by-step adult guidance\
- Safety considerations\
- EYFS tie-in explanation\
- Clearly defined ethos adaptations (substantive, not decorative)

Activities are written to: - Reduce practitioner cognitive load\
- Provide observation clarity\
- Support documentation confidence\
- Stand up to inspection scrutiny

No unnecessary extensions or "bonus extras" that devalue premium
content.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### Packs

Activity packs: - Thematically grouped\
- Age-coherent\
- Structured progression where appropriate\
- Balanced across prime and specific areas

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### Subscription

Subscription provides: - Full access to gated activity library\
- Ongoing content updates\
- Structured filtering (themes, ethos, seasons, development focus)

Low barrier to entry.\
Designed for recurring retention, not high-ticket extraction.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 5. Business Model Constraints

-   Keep fixed costs minimal\
-   Use no-code or low-cost infrastructure where possible\
-   Avoid unnecessary SaaS stacking\
-   Automate wherever feasible

Revenue streams: 1. Subscription (core recurring income)\
2. Individual activity purchases\
3. Activity pack bundles\
4. Long-term: B2B consultancy / workshops\
5. Long-term: AI activity generator (enterprise tier concept)

Pricing philosophy: - Accessible\
- Repeat-friendly\
- Scalable\
- Sustainable without high churn dependency

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 6. Brand & Tone Principles (Content Writing)

Writing style must be: - Clear\
- Calm\
- Structured\
- Non-performative\
- Non-preachy\
- Evidence-aware

Avoid: - Overly whimsical tone\
- Teacher guilt language\
- Parenting judgement language\
- Buzzwords\
- Empty ethos references

Ethos adaptations must: - Change environment or adult role meaningfully\
- Demonstrate philosophical consistency\
- Avoid shallow one-line summaries

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 7. Visual Direction

Imagery must: - Reflect real early years environments\
- Use natural light where possible\
- Avoid stock-photo feel\
- Avoid repeated children across activities\
- Follow enforced stylistic framework (16:9 hero ratio maintained)

Imagery supports credibility, not aesthetic indulgence.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 8. Automation & Workflow Principles

Current pipeline pain points: - Apple Notes → manual transfer → ChatGPT
→ Google Sheets → Webflow CMS

Target state: - Single content entry point\
- Automated transformation into structured activity article\
- Automated population into CMS draft\
- Minimal manual reformatting

AI usage principles: - AI supports structured generation\
- AI must follow activity structure guide\
- AI must not invent regulatory claims\
- AI must not hallucinate developmental science\
- AI outputs must remain EYFS-aligned

Long-term concept: - Internal RAG-powered activity generator\
- Eventually exposed as paid enterprise tool\
- Recipe-based structured generation

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 9. Quality Control Rules

Every activity must pass: 1. EYFS Alignment Check\
2. Age Appropriateness Check\
3. Safeguarding Awareness Check\
4. Materials Realism Check\
5. Ethos Depth Check\
6. SEO Title Structure Check\
7. Visual Consistency Check

No activity is published if: - It feels generic\
- It could exist unchanged on Pinterest\
- It lacks observation clarity\
- It adds no professional value

## 12. Decision Filter

When making decisions, ask: - Does this reduce cognitive load for
practitioners?\
- Does this align with EYFS statutory expectations?\
- Does this strengthen professional credibility?\
- Does this increase automation or add friction?\
- Does this protect low-cost scalability?\
- Does this feel like a professional planning tool, not a hobby blog?

If it fails those tests, it does not ship.
