# **Main Sections (CMS Order)**

## **Section Order Lock (CSV)**

To support CMS import/export, activities must keep sections in the **same order as the CMS CSV export**. Ignore internal CMS-only columns (images, flags, linked product), but preserve the order of all content sections.

### Required section order:

1. Title of the Activity
2. Themes
3. Activity Summary
4. Learning Objectives
5. Observation Cues
6. Linked Materials (preferred) / Materials (fallback)
7. Step-by-Step Guidance
8. Adult Role
9. Age Adaptation: 0–12 months (Little Learners)
10. Age Adaptation: 12–24 months (Early Explorers)
11. Age Adaptation: 2 years (Budding Adventurers)

14. Space Required

# **Activity Summary**

A short contextual overview of the activity.

### Purpose:

- Expands slightly on the title
- Clarifies the focus and experience
- Does not reveal gated instructional detail

### Constraints:

- 2–4 sentences maximum
- No step-by-step detail
- No full explanation of setup
- No ethos depth
- No safety specifics

This is positioning, not instruction.

---

# **Learning Objectives**

Clear developmental intent linked to specific [[The EYFS seven areas of learning & development]].

### Focus on:

- What children may practise, explore or develop through this activity
- The developmental progression being supported, not outcomes promised
- Which EYFS areas of learning are engaged (name them explicitly)
- Language that is honest about potential, not prescriptive about results

### Constraints:

- Do not embed observation cues here — these are now a separate section
- Do not use outcome-led language ("children will learn to…") — use exploratory language ("children may begin to…", "this activity supports…")
- Must be clearly tied to at least one named EYFS area
- 2–4 sentences or a short structured paragraph

---

# **Observation Cues**

Practical, in-the-moment guidance to help practitioners notice meaningful developmental moments during the activity.

### Purpose:

- Reduce the cognitive load of real-time observation
- Give practitioners specific things to look for, listen to, or notice
- Support documentation confidence and Ofsted-ready evidence gathering

### Focus on:

- What the adult might hear (language, sounds, vocalisations)
- What the adult might see (grip, posture, movement, facial expression, peer interaction)
- What the adult might notice over time (returning to the activity, increased confidence, shifting approach)

### Constraints:

- Written as practical prompts, not theoretical observation theory
- Should feel usable in the moment, not retrospective
- 3–6 specific cues, written in plain, professional language
- Do not duplicate content from Learning Objectives
- Do not use vague catch-all language ("notice how children engage") — be specific

Preferred over generic material lists. Reference [[Materials list]] wherever possible. Use consistent naming conventions.

### Materials must be:

- Realistic for settings
- Safety-aware
- Age-appropriate
- Specific (no vague categories such as "various items")

### Must include:

- Environment preparation
- How materials are presented
- Adult interaction woven naturally into flow
- Clear transitions where relevant

Guidance must reduce practitioner interpretation. Avoid decorative language.

Adult role should feel intentional and professional, not instinctive or casual.

# **Ethos Adaptations**

### Include only:

- [[Montessori Ethos Adaptation – Model Skill Reference|Montessori]]
- [[Reggio Emilia Ethos Adaptation — Model Skill Reference|Reggio Emilia]]
- [[Forest School Research Ethos Adaptation — Model Skill Reference|Forest School]]
- [[Steiner (Waldorf) Ethos Adaptation — Model-Facing Skill Spec|Steiner/Waldorf]]

Adaptations are practical applications, not philosophy summaries.

### Each ethos adaptation must:

- Describe at least three distinct adult decisions
- Include a change to environment or materials
- Include a shift in adult behaviour or language
- Describe what the adult observes and responds to

### Adaptations should usually be two short paragraphs:

- Paragraph one: environmental and material changes
- Paragraph two: adult interaction and observation shifts

If an adaptation could be reduced to a single sentence without losing meaning, it is too shallow and must be rewritten.

If followed, the adaptation should make the activity look and feel meaningfully different.

# **Preview Content**

An intentionally abridged version of the full activity. This section is written as part of every activity and populates the CMS preview field directly.

### Purpose:

- Demonstrates professional depth to non-subscribers
- Signals structure and quality
- Acts as a teaser for full gated content

### Constraints:

- Must withhold full procedural clarity
- Must not include complete setup logic
- Must not include full ethos adaptations
- Must not reference subscription or payment
- Must not feel like a summary — it should feel like the beginning of something useful, cut short

Preview content should feel useful but incomplete. It must not undermine gated value.

---

# **Non-Negotiable Principles**

- Do not give away full gated instructional value in Summary or Preview.
- Do not weaken professional credibility for brevity.
- Do not inflate word count outside of high-value sections (Ethos, Adult Role, EYFS explanation).
- All content must align with EYFS statutory expectations and safeguarding requirements.

> ❗ If the structure is not followed exactly, the activity must be rewritten before publication.
