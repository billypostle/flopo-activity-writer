# Early Years Resources

## Visual Style Guide v2.0 – Production Specification

This document defines mandatory production constraints for all generated imagery.
It is not a brand statement. It is an enforcement framework.

All future image generation must comply with these specifications.

---

# 1. Global Production Defaults (Non-Optional)

Unless explicitly overridden, every image must follow these baseline settings.

## 1.1 Camera & Framing

- Perspective equivalent to 35mm full-frame lens.
- Camera positioned at child eye-level OR maximum 20cm above.
- No ultra-wide distortion.
- No telephoto compression look.
- Frame must include environmental context (no floating subjects).

## 1.2 Depth of Field

- Moderate depth of field.
- Subject clearly readable.
- Background still legible.
- No extreme blur.
- No artificial portrait-mode cutout edges.

## 1.3 Lighting

- Single-direction natural window-led light source.
- Soft shadow edges.
- No overhead spotlight effect.
- No multi-direction studio fill.
- No hard-edged cast shadows.

## 1.4 Tonal Treatment

- Slight warmth (subtle, not orange).
- Muted contrast (avoid deep blacks).
- Highlights gently rolled off.
- Skin tones neutral and realistic.

## 1.5 Grain & Optical Imperfection

- Fine organic film grain visible at 100% zoom.
- Grain evenly distributed.
- Slight reduction in digital micro-contrast.
- Subtle natural vignette (barely perceptible).
- No hyper-sharp edge definition.

If image appears hyper-clean, over-sharpened or digitally glossy, it fails.

---

# 2. Image Type Enforcement

Each image must be declared as one of the following:

- Hero (EYFS Core Execution)
- Montessori Adaptation
- Forest School Adaptation
- Reggio Emilia Adaptation
- Steiner / Waldorf Adaptation

Images must never:

- Combine ethos elements.
- Visually compare two ethos in one frame.
- Present composite or split layouts.
- Reuse identical framing across image types.

---

# 3. Structural Variation Requirements (Mandatory)

Ethos adaptation images MUST differ structurally from the hero image.

All of the following must change:

- Different children OR no children.
- Different camera angle (minimum \~15° shift horizontally).
- Different crop distance (medium vs closer detail vs wider context).
- Different working surface (table vs floor vs outdoor ground).
- Different material arrangement.

If the ethos image mirrors subject placement, body positioning or hand placement from the hero, it fails.

---

# 4. Scene Template System

All images must align to one of the following scene structures.

## Template A – Tabletop Activity

- Children seated at child-height table.
- Materials spread realistically.
- Natural interaction mid-process.

## Template B – Floor-Based Exploration

- Activity on floor mat or low surface.
- More open spatial framing.
- Movement visible.

## Template C – Outdoor Natural Context

- Uneven natural ground.
- Real weather conditions.
- Layered clothing.
- Environmental depth.

## Template D – Material Focus

- No children required.
- Focus on material setup.
- Clear environmental context.
- Slightly closer framing.

Hero and ethos images should not default to the same template unless structurally altered.

---

# 5. Material Behaviour Specification

Materials must appear physically believable.

- Paint must not appear hyper-glossy.
- Reduce specular highlights on plastic.
- Prefer matte surface rendering.
- Paper may show slight curl or lift.
- Stamps may show uneven pressure.
- Surfaces may show light use marks.

At least one minor imperfection must be visible:

- Small paint smudge.
- Slight fabric crease.
- Natural wood grain irregularity.
- Subtle misalignment in print pattern.

Perfection indicates artificiality.

---

# 6. Environment Specification

Environment must:

- Be a realistic UK early years setting.
- Include child-height furniture.
- Avoid showroom perfection.
- Avoid sterile clinical minimalism.
- Avoid influencer-style interior styling.

No luxury curated toy displays.
No design-magazine composition.
No unrealistic architectural lighting.

---

# 7. Child Representation Constraints

Children must:

- Match stated age range accurately.
- Display developmentally appropriate grip and posture.
- Wear realistic everyday clothing.
- Show natural diversity without staged tokenism.

Never:

- Perfect mirrored poses.
- Identical hand positioning across frames.
- Overly posed expressions.
- Direct camera engagement.

Skin must retain natural texture.
No airbrushed smoothness.

---

# 8. Adult Representation Constraints

If adults are present:

- Body positioned at child level.
- Open posture.
- No dominating stance.
- No direct camera awareness.

Adults must not become compositional anchors.

---

# 9. Safeguarding Enforcement

Images must not depict:

- Unsafe material use.
- Choking hazards in under-3 contexts.
- Overcrowded spacing.
- Unrealistic supervision.

Furniture must match child scale.
Outdoor scenes must imply safe supervision.

---

# 10. Anti-Digital Failure Conditions

An image fails if it shows:

- Bilateral symmetry in subject placement.
- Identical compositional layout to another image in the series.
- Excessively saturated primary colours.
- Hyper-clean surfaces.
- Unrealistically smooth skin.
- Edge-to-edge sharpness.
- Unrealistically perfect lighting balance.
- Obvious compositing behaviour.
- Identical children across ethos frames.

---

# 11. Prompt Construction Rule

Prompts must only specify:

1. Image Type
2. Age Range
3. Activity Description
4. Materials (exact list)
5. Scene Template (A, B, C or D)

All other behaviour is governed by this document.

If output deviates from this framework, regenerate with correction.

---

# 12. Quality Control Checklist

Before approving an image, confirm:

- Structural variation requirements met.
- Grain visible at 100% zoom.
- No compositional symmetry.
- Materials appear matte and believable.
- At least one subtle imperfection present.
- Children age-accurate.
- Lighting consistent with global defaults.

If more than one failure condition is present, discard and regenerate.

---

This specification overrides all prior versions.
All future image generation must comply with v2.0.
