Style Guide v2.0 — QC Checklist
Awaiting image
Generate an image to activate the checklist. After generation, manually review each item and mark pass or fail based on what you observe in the preview.
§1 Camera, Optics & Grain
–
Camera at child eye-level (max 20cm above)
35mm equivalent. No ultra-wide distortion or telephoto compression.
–
Moderate depth of field
Subject sharp, background legible. No extreme blur or portrait-mode cutout edges.
–
Organic film grain visible
Fine grain evenly distributed. No hyper-sharp edges, no digital gloss. Subtle vignette.
–
Environmental context included
No floating subjects. Setting visible and legible around the activity.
§1 Lighting & Tonal Treatment
–
Single-direction natural window light
Soft shadow edges. No overhead spotlight. No multi-direction studio fill.
–
Muted contrast, subtle warmth
No deep blacks. Highlights gently rolled off. Skin tones neutral and realistic. Not orange.
§2 Image Type Compliance
–
Single ethos only — no mixing
No composite or split layouts. No elements from a different ethos visible in frame.
–
Structural variation from hero (ethos images)
Different camera angle (≥15°), different crop, different surface, different material arrangement, different children.
§5 Material Behaviour
–
Materials appear matte and physically believable
No hyper-glossy paint. Reduced specular on plastic. Matte surface rendering preferred.
–
At least one visible minor imperfection
Paint smudge, paper curl, fabric crease, wood grain, misalignment, uneven stamp pressure.
§6 Environment
–
Realistic UK early years setting
Child-height furniture. No showroom perfection. No sterile minimalism. No influencer styling.
§7 Child Representation
–
Children match stated age range
Developmentally appropriate grip and posture for the selected age band.
–
Natural diversity — no staged tokenism
Realistic everyday clothing. No matching outfits. Authentic range of skin tones.
–
No posed expressions or camera awareness
No direct camera engagement. No perfect mirrored poses. No identical hand positioning.
–
Natural skin texture — no airbrushing
Skin must retain realistic texture. No smoothed or digitally cleaned skin.
§10 Anti-Digital Failure Conditions
–
No bilateral symmetry in subject placement
Composition must feel organic, not centred or perfectly balanced.
–
No excessively saturated primary colours
Colours should feel muted and natural, not vibrant or toy-catalogue.
–
No obvious compositing or CGI behaviour
No edge halos, no mismatched shadows, no artificial depth separation.
